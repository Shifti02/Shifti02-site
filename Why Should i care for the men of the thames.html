Analysis

Why Should I Care for the men in the thames

This poem expresses a strong sentiment of defiance against oppression and a yearning for freedom. The speaker rejects the constraints imposed by their birthplace and the influence of those who would seek to control them. The references to the River Thames and the Ohio River symbolize the speaker's rejection of their past and their aspiration for a better future.

Why should I care for the men of thames

Or the cheating waves of charter'd streams

Or shrink at the little blasts of fear

That the hireling blows into my ear

Tho born on the cheating banks of Thames

Tho his waters bathed my infant limbs

The Ohio shall wash his stains from me

I was born a slave but I go to be free.
