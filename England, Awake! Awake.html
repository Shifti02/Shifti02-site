Analysis

England, Awake! Awake

Jerusalem here represents an idealized vision of spiritual and moral purity. By referring to Jerusalem as England's sister, Blake suggests a deep, intrinsic connection between England and this ideal state. Blake evokes a nostalgic vision of a time when England was in harmony with divine principles. The gentle movement of feet on hills and valleys, and the gates beholding Zion's ways, suggest a period of peace, joy, and spiritual enlightenment.

England! awake! awake! awake!

Jerusalem thy Sister calls!

Why wilt thou sleep the sleep of death

And close her from thy ancient walls?

Thy hills and valleys felt her feet

Gently upon their bosoms move:

Thy gates beheld sweet Zion's ways:

Then was a time of joy and love.

And now the time returns again:

Our souls exult, and London's towers

Receive the Lamb of God to dwell

In England's green and pleasant bowers.
