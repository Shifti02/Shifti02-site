Analysis

Holy Thursday(Innocence)

This poem, written during the Romantic era, celebrates the beauty and purity of children. The narrator describes a group of children walking in a procession, their faces innocent and their clothing vibrant. The beadles, or church officials, walk before them, their wands symbolizing authority and guidance.

Twas on a Holy Thursday their innocent faces clean

The children walking two & two in red & blue & green

Grey headed beadles walked before with wands as white as snow

Till into the high dome of Pauls they like Thames waters flow

O what a multitude they seemed these flowers of London town

Seated in companies they sit with radiance all their own

The hum of multitudes was there but multitudes of lambs

Thousands of little boys & girls raising their innocent hands

Now like a mighty wind they raise to heaven the voice of song

Or like harmonious thunderings the seats of heaven among

Beneath them sit the aged men wise guardians of the poor

Then cherish pity, lest you drive an angel from your door
