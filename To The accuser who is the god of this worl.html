Analysis

To The accuser who is the god of this world

This poem, a powerful indictment against religious hypocrisy, deconstructs the authority of the "Accuser" figure, who is likened to the "Dunce" and "Satan." Blake questions the validity of religious institutions, suggesting that they have corrupted the fundamental principles of Christianity. The poem's biting sarcasm and satirical tone echo the political and social upheaval of Blake's time, marked by the rise of both religious fundamentalism and revolutionary fervor.

Truly My Satan thou art but a Dunce

And dost not know the Garment from the Man

Every Harlot was a Virgin once

Nor canst thou ever change Kate into Nan

Tho thou art Worship'd by the Names Divine

Of Jesus & Jehovah thou art still

The Son of Morn in weary Nights decline

The lost Travellers Dream under the Hill
