The Laocoön

William Blake's "Laocoön" is a fascinating and complex work that combines visual art with a rich tapestry of inscriptions and commentary, reflecting Blake's unique religious and philosophical beliefs. Created around 1826, the piece is a pen and ink etching that features the famous Hellenistic sculpture of Laocoön and his sons being attacked by serpents, overlaid with Blake's handwritten notes and aphorisms
