Analysis

Piping Down the valleys wild

The speaker encounters a child on a cloud who requests a song about a lamb. The speaker's song brings the child both laughter and tears, prompting him to ask the speaker to write down the songs for all children to enjoy. The poem is characterized by its simple, yet evocative language and its use of repetition. The refrain "Pipe a song about a lamb" creates a sense of rhythm and movement, while the repetition of the word "happy" conveys the speaker's sense of joy.

Piping down the valleys wild,

Piping songs of pleasant glee,

On a cloud I saw a child,

And he laughing said to me:

'Pipe a song about a Lamb!'

So I piped with merry cheer.

'Piper, pipe that song again;'

So I piped: he wept to hear.

'Drop thy pipe, thy happy pipe;

Sing thy songs of happy cheer!'

So I sung the same again,

While he wept with joy to hear.

'Piper, sit thee down and write

In a book that all may read.'

So he vanish'd from my sight;

And I pluck'd a hollow reed,

And I made a rural pen,

And I stain'd the water clear,

And I wrote my happy songs

Every child may joy to hear.
