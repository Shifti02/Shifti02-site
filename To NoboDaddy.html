To Nobodaddy

Nobodaddy. the father of jealousy who hides himself in clouds and loves "hanging and drawing and quartering" has recieved general critical agreement as to the significance of his name. This Desctructive divinity manifests himslef elsewhere as Urizen, Winter, the Will. But Nobodaddy, is a close anagram of the name of a character who appears in two of Blake's favorite Biblical books: Abaddon, anagrammatized Abaddon becomes Nobodad(the angel of the bottomless pit).

Why art thou silent & invisible

Father of jealousy

Why dost thou hide thyself in clouds

From every searching Eye

Why darkness & obscurity

In all thy words & laws

That none dare eat the fruit but from

The wily serpents jaws

Or is it because Secresy

gains females loud applause
