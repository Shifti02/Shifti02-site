Analysis

The Invocation

Blake Invokes the daughters of Beulah, the Muses who inspire the poet's song, to aid him in recording the journey of the immortal poet Milton. The passage speaks of Milton's journey through realms of terror and mild moonlit beauty. The Muses are called upon to descend down the nerves of the poet's arm, then Blake incorporates biblical imagery and themes into the passage, referencing Jesus as the "image of the Invisible God" and his sacrifice as an offering and atonement for eternal death.
