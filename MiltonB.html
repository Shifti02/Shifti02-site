Milton: A poem in two books

Blake presents John Milton, the author of "Paradise Lost," as a revolutionary and visionary poet. He sees Milton as a kindred spirit who rebels against the oppressive forces of his time, particularly the political and religious authoritiesHe portrays Milton as a prophet and visionary who receives divine inspiration from spiritual beings.Albion's fall from grace parallels the biblical story of Adam and Eve's expulsion from Eden.The poem draws upon a wide range of sources, including Greek and Roman mythology, Christian theology, and Blake's own idiosyncratic mythology. These allusions enrich the poem's symbolic texture.
