Analysis

The Sword sung on the barren heath

"The Sword sung on the barren heath" is a poem by William Blake that explores themes of conflict, desolation, and the destructive nature of war. The imagery and language Blake employs evoke a sense of foreboding and devastation

The sword sung on the barren heath

The sickle in the fruitful field

The sword he sung a song of death

But could not make the sickle yield
