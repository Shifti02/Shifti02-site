Analysis

I Saw a chapel

The Poem depicts a scene where a beautiful chapel is desecrated by the presence of a snake. This Is the moral decay and hypocrisy in the church with its beautiful exterior but in the interior corruption, where the speaker's rejection of the chapel and decision to lay in the swine symbolizes a moral decay and spiritual degradation to answer the corruption.

I was angry with my friend:

I saw a chapel all of gold

That none did dare to enter in,

And many weeping stood without,

Weeping, mourning, worshipping.

I saw a serpent rise between

The white pillars of the door,

And he forc'd and forc'd and forc'd,

Down the golden hinges tore.

And along the pavement sweet,

Set with pearls and rubies bright,

All his slimy length he drew

Till upon the altar white

Vomiting his poison out

On the bread and on the wine.

So I turn'd into a sty

And laid me down among the swine.
