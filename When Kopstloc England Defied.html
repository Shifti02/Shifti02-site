Analysis

When Klopstock England Defied

The poem presents a humorous and exaggerated account of William Blake's defiance against the German poet Klopstock, who criticized England. Blake's vehement response, represented as an oath that shakes Heaven, prompts a chaotic reaction in nature and the underworld. The use of hyperbole and playful language distinguishes the poem from Blake's more serious works.
