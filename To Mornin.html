Analysis

To Morning

The Poem is an ode to the dawn, personified as a "holy virgin." The poem represents a typical invocation of the period, featuring allusions to classical mythology and a reverential tone.

O holy virgin! clad in purest white,

Unlock heav'n's golden gates, and issue forth;

Awake the dawn that sleeps in heaven; let light

Rise from the chambers of the east, and bring

The honey'd dew that cometh on waking day.

O radiant morning, salute the sun

Rous'd like a huntsman to the chase, and with

Thy buskin'd feet appear upon our hills.
